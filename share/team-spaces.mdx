Why team agents? Because some workflows affect the **whole team** — and then everyone should be able to access the agent, edit it, and collaborate on it. That's the purpose of a team space.

## Personal vs. team

- **Personal space** — your private agents, for experiments and your own productivity.
- **Team space** — a full workspace for the team: its own prompt box (agents built there are team agents from the start), its own **Agents**, **Templates**, and **Skills** tabs, its own connections and [budget](/admin/credits-and-usage).

## Connections in a team

Connect a tool once in the team, and **every team agent can use it** — including agents teammates build later.

<Warning>
  Use [service accounts](/admin/connected-apps-management), not personal logins — a tool connected in a team is usable by every agent in that team.
</Warning>

## Promote a personal agent to the team

Click **Copy to team** on your agent. The team gets its own copy — **your original stays unchanged** in your personal space. That's useful: keep iterating privately, and promote again when you have a better version.
